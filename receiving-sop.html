Goods Receiving — Photo Checklist

5 required photo types on every delivery
1

Goods Photo

Requirements
  • Photograph all incoming goods upon arrival.
  • Items must not be covered or obstructed — product type and quantity must be clearly identifiable in the photo.
2

Delivery Note Photo

Requirements
  • All items on the delivery note must be ticked off before signing.
  • Receiver's name must be written on the note before taking the photo.
3

Frozen Goods Temperature Photo

Requirements
  • Frozen goods temperature must be below −15°C.
  • 3 or fewer frozen product types — randomly check and photograph 1 item.
  • More than 3 frozen product types — randomly check and photograph 3 items.
⚠️ Temperature probe must be visible and reading must be clearly legible in the photo.
4

Re-weighing Photo

When to apply
  • Applies to fresh produce sold by weight where the packaging does not state a specific quantity — primarily Pak Choi.
How to photograph
  • Place the item including its packaging box on the digital scale.
  • Photograph the scale display showing the weight in grams.
5

Quality Check Photo

What to check
  • Cabbage — check for excessive black spots.
  • Pak Choi — check for excessive rotten or damaged leaves.
  • All other fresh produce — check for any obvious quality issues.
⚠️ If any quality issue is found — photograph and report to management on the same day as delivery, without delay.
